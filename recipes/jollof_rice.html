Jollof Rice

A beloved West African classic! This one-pot rice dish is cooked in a rich tomato and pepper sauce, seasoned with spices, and often served with fried plantains or grilled chicken. It’s a staple at Nigerian parties and family gatherings

Ingredients

  • 2 cups long-grain parboiled rice
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2-3 tomatoes, blended (or 1 can of crushed tomatoes)
  • 1 red bell pepper, blended
  • 1-2 Scotch bonnet peppers (adjust to taste)
  • 2-3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder (optional)
  • 4 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: protein of choice (chicken, beef, or fish)

Steps for preparing Jollof Rice

  1. Rinse the rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large pot, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and sauté until translucent.
  3. Add the blended tomatoes, red bell pepper, and Scotch bonnet peppers to the pot. Cook for about 10-15 minutes until the sauce thickens and the oil begins to separate.
  4. Stir in the minced garlic, thyme, curry powder (if using), salt, and pepper. Cook for another 2-3 minutes.
  5. Add the rinsed rice to the pot and stir well to coat the rice with the sauce.
  6. Pour in the chicken or vegetable broth, bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low. Cover and simmer for about 20-25 minutes or until the rice is cooked and has absorbed most of the liquid.
  7. If using protein, you can add it during the last 10 minutes of cooking to allow it to heat through.
  8. Fluff the rice with a fork before serving. Enjoy your delicious Jollof Rice!
