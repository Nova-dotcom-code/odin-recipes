Beef Suya Skewers

A spicy, smoky street food favorite made with thinly sliced beef marinated in a peanut-based spice mix. It's grilled to perfection and served with sliced onions and tomatoes. Suya is bold, flavorful, and deeply rooted in Nigerian food culture

Ingredients

  • 1 pound beef (sirloin or flank steak), thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons ground peanuts (or peanut butter)
  • 1 tablespoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • Wooden skewers (soaked in water for 30 minutes if grilling)

Steps for prepare Beef Suya Skewers

  1. In a bowl, mix the ground peanuts, paprika, cayenne pepper, garlic powder, onion powder, and salt.
  2. Add the vegetable oil to the spice mix and stir to form a paste.
  3. Rub the spice paste all over the thinly sliced beef, ensuring each piece is well coated. Marinate for at least 30 minutes (or overnight for more flavor).
  4. Preheat your grill or grill pan over medium-high heat.
  5. Thread the marinated beef onto the soaked skewers.
  6. Grill the skewers for about 3-4 minutes on each side or until cooked to your desired doneness.
  7. Serve hot with sliced onions and tomatoes on the side.
  8. Enjoy your homemade beef suya!
